What's true understanding?

Recently I’ve taken a class in UoA called “Self-Leadership”, which has given me a great deal of food for thought. It was somewhat novel and unfamiliar to me, and I mistook it for a typical self-success course.
But the course content turned out to be surprisingly insightful. I’ve learned the most fundamental practice of self-leadership: reflection. There is no secret formula or magic behind it. It simply offers me a quiet chance to pause, slow down, and reflect on my inner self.

TL;DR

  • The illusion of understanding is a common trap in learning, where we feel like we understand something but lack genuine mastery.
  • Use Feynman’s Method to check if you understand the concept.

Observation

These days I spent plenty of time diving into CSAPP, a classic book in computer science. Over the past few years in my work, I always intended to read this book thoroughly, but never got around to it.

What I found interesting is that I acted differently. I somehow took on two distinct approaches once I began learning basic concepts such as Virtual Memory and Set Associative Caches.

To summarize, I understand Set Associative Caches better than Virtual Memory. Meanwhile, reading the chapter on Virtual Memory felt more enjoyable and rewarding. In contrast, studying the other topic is quite challenging and “full of dopamine”.

Inflection

Why?
It is an issue with the illusion of understanding. It is recognition, yet not genuine mastery.

When I closed the book, and looked back at the whole picture of Virtual Memory. I asked myself, but I got stuck over and over again. I couldn’t clarify the details of the concept, and I came up with some new questions. It ended up the knowledge is like a cyclone, it comes and goes, leaving a mess.

I think the reason is that I used to use Virtual Memory a lot in my work. Therefore, I just read the concepts happily without stop thinking more about it. There was a voice in mind said “Yeah, that’s it. The layout is just like that. The underlying mechanism is just like that.”. I was so satisfied that I had learnt so much again. (Maybe after several days, I could be “happy” once more). 😔

In terms of Set Associative Caches, it’s not a huge knowledge, so I break it down into smaller parts and draw diagrams to help me understand it better. I somehow map the diagrams in my mind, and start explaining it to myself. Every time I get stuck by something that I can’t make it clear, I just go back to the book and read it again. Eventually, I really master it! I can spot connections between it and my existing knowledge. 😁

Implication && Action

I may have tried these methods consciously or unconsciously before. But now I need to define them clearly and supervise myself to see if I can put them into practice 👊.

  1. Thinking about how to articulate the ideas without opening the book. Actually it’s Feynman’s Method. Personally, I can understand about 90% of new knowledge after using the Feynman Method.
  2. Drawing diagrams to help me understand the concepts better.
  3. Break down the knowledge into smaller parts, making it easier to take action.
